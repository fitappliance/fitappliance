How to measure this fridge cavity

Use these three views before you shortlist appliances: width, height, and depth all need to pass with ventilation clearance.

1. Prepare a tape measure and notepad

Use a rigid tape measure and record each dimension in millimetres. Measure from fixed cabinet edges, not from trim pieces.

2. Measure cavity width

Measure the internal cavity width at the front and the back, then use the smaller value. This prevents side-wall taper from being missed.

3. Measure cavity height

Measure from finished floor level to the underside of overhead cabinetry at both left and right sides, then keep the smaller value.

4. Measure cavity depth

Measure from the back wall to the cabinet front edge. Include any skirting, power point cover, or plumbing protrusion in the usable depth.

5. Apply ventilation clearance before purchase

Compare your cavity measurements against appliance dimensions plus manufacturer clearances for side, rear, and top ventilation.
