# Commission Factory Application Manifest

## Business description

FitAppliance is an Australian appliance-fit utility that helps households choose appliances that physically fit their measured space before they buy. Users enter cavity dimensions or old-appliance dimensions, then FitAppliance compares those measurements against product dimensions, verified retailer links, PDF evidence where available, practical clearance buffers, doorway constraints and category-specific installation notes.

The core value proposition is reduction of appliance return risk. Large appliances are expensive to deliver, return and reinstall. FitAppliance focuses on high-intent purchase moments where the user already knows their available space and needs a defensible shortlist of products that can plausibly fit. The product is built around a fail-closed data philosophy: when dimensions, clearances or source documents are ambiguous, we withhold verified-fit trust signals rather than overstate certainty.

FitAppliance is not a coupon site, review farm or generic product directory. It is a practical decision tool for Australian fridges, washing machines, dishwashers and dryers. Retailer links are presented as the next step after a fit calculation, not as intrusive advertising.

## Traffic acquisition strategy

Our traffic acquisition strategy is SEO-led and intent-led. FitAppliance targets long-tail searches where users are trying to solve a real installation problem, such as whether a specific fridge fits a 900 mm cavity, which dishwashers fit a tight under-bench opening, or which current models can replace an old appliance with known dimensions.

The site generates structured, useful pages around appliance dimensions, brand clearances, fit-check scenarios, comparison pages and methodology content. These pages are designed for users with immediate purchase intent rather than broad browsing traffic. We also maintain transparent methodology, privacy, terms, contact and affiliate-disclosure pages to support search trust, partner review and user confidence.

## Audience and conversion profile

FitAppliance visitors are typically close to purchase. They have a measured cavity, a shortlist of models, or an old appliance they need to replace. That creates a high conversion-rate profile because outbound clicks happen after the user has reduced a major purchase risk.

The highest-intent flows are:

- Cavity-dimension searches for fridges, dishwashers, washing machines and dryers.
- Old-to-new replacement searches using an existing appliance size.
- Side-by-side comparisons of models that differ in depth, height, clearance or delivery risk.
- Product cards with verified retailer product-page links and source transparency.

## Affiliate integration approach

Affiliate links are clearly labelled and treated as a natural continuation of the fit result. The site discloses that FitAppliance is reader-supported and may earn an affiliate commission when users buy through retailer links. Commission does not alter the fit calculation or override the fail-closed data rules.
